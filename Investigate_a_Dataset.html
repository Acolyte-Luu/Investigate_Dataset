Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset gives us the records of patients and their appointments over a period of time. My analysis is to uncover the main contributing factors, to patients not attending their scheduled appointments.

Dataset columns and description

PatientId - Patient identification.

AppointmentID - Appointment Number

Gender - Gender of patient(Male or Female).

ScheduledDay - The day patient scheduled their appointment.

AppointmentDay - The day of actual visit to the doctor.

Age - Patient Age

Neighbourhood - Hospital location.

Scholarship - Whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia(0 is no & 1 is Yes).

Hipertension - Whether or not the patient has hypertension(0 is no & 1 is Yes).

Diabetes - Whether or not the patient has diabetes(0 is no & 1 is Yes).

Alcoholism - Whether or not the patient has alcoholism(0 is no & 1 is Yes).

Handicap - Whether or not the patient has handicaps(on scale of 0 to 4,where each number indicates the number of handicaps)

SMS_received = Whether or not the patient received SMS reminding patient of scheduled appointment(0 is no & 1 is Yes)

No-show = Whether or not the patient attended the scheduled appointment(Yes for missed appointment , No for did not miss appointment)

Question(s) for Analysis¶

Question 1. Does the gender of the patient affect appointment attendance rate?

Question 2. Does receiving SMS's affect the appointment attendance rate?

Question 3. Is age a factor in determining appointment attendance?

Question 4. Is being on the Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia contribute to appointment attendance?

Question 5. Does havinga handicap affect the attendance rate?

#Create a new column age_group based on the values in the column age
def categorize_age(row):
    if 0 <= row['Age'] <= 12:
        value = 'Child'
    elif 12 < row['Age'] <= 20:
        value = 'Adolescent'
    elif 20 < row['Age'] <= 60:
        value = 'Adult'
    else:
        value = 'Senior Citizen'
    return value
medical_appointments_df['Age_Group'] = medical_appointments_df.apply(lambda row: categorize_age(row),axis=1)

#Function to plot percentage of the various columns against the Missed_Scheduled_Appointment column
def get_percentage(df,x_col,hue_col,title):
    df1 = medical_appointments_df.groupby(x_col)[hue_col].value_counts(normalize=True)
    df1 = df1.mul(100)
    df1 = df1.rename('percent').reset_index()
    g = sns.catplot(x=x_col,y='percent',hue=hue_col,kind='bar',data=df1).set(title=title)
    g.ax.set_ylim(0,100)    
    for p in g.ax.patches:
        txt = str(p.get_height().round(2)) + '%'
        txt_x = p.get_x() 
        txt_y = p.get_height()
        g.ax.text(txt_x,txt_y,txt)

Conclusions¶

From the above visualizations and tables the questions posed can be answered with a high degree of certainty.

Question 1. Does the gender of the patient affect appointment attendance rate?

Answer Gender has no correlation to patients attending their appointments. The percentage of both men and women missing appointments is almost the same.

Question 2. Does receiving SMS's affect the appointment attendance rate?

Answer Receiving SMS's proir to appointment dates did not also significantly affect the appointment attendance rates. Those who did and those who did not attended appointments at closely the same rate.

Question 3. Is age a factor in determining appointment attendance?

Answer Age has direct correlation with attendance rates. The youngest age group(0-12 years old - Child) and the oldest age group(above 60 years - Senior Citizen) have the highest attendance rates. Adolescents(13-20 years) have the lowest attendance rate, closely followed by adults(21-60 years)

Question 4. Is being on the Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia contribute to appointment attendance?

Answer Being on the Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia has no direct correlation with attendance rates. The rate of people on the welfare program and not on the welfare program that attended their appointments were fairly the same.

Question 5. Does having a handicap affect the attendance rate?

Answer Handicap does not have a direct correlation with attendance rates.
